## Description

<p><strong><span style="font-size: 18pt;">Deleted Email Recovery</span></strong></p>
<p>By default, every email deleted from your OWA mailbox goes through the following process:</p>
<ol><li>The email is moved to your Deleted Items folder.</li><li>If you purge or remove items from your Deleted Items folder they will be moved to the Dumpster where they are still recoverable.</li><li>The Dumpster only holds email message for 14 days once they&#39;ve been removed from Deleted Items.</li></ol>
<p>Deleted email can be recovered from the Deleted Items folder or the Dumpster. Once it has been permanently deleted from the dumpster it can not be recovered.</p>
<p><b>Note:</b> Desktop clients (e.g., Outlook, Entourage, Apple Mail) may be configured to use a local trash system or be configured to purge email from the Deleted Items folder more quickly than 14 days.</p>
<p>Deleted Email Recovery is only available in OWA or Outlook.</p>
<p><strong><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Delete Email</span></strong></p>
<p>When you are ready to delete email from your Inbox, select the email by clicking on it once and then either:</p>
<ul><li>select the Delete icon on the OWA Toolbar,<br />OR</li><li>right-click and select <b>Delete</b>.<br /><em>Result:</em> The email is moved to Deleted Items</li></ul>
<h2><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Permanently Delete Email from Deleted Items</span></h2>
<ol><li>Access your mailbox and navigate to <b>Deleted Items</b>.</li><li>Select the email you want to delete by clicking once on the email to highlight it.
<ul><li>To select a range of emails, single-click on the first email in the range, press down the Shift key, and single-click on the last email in the range.</li><li>To select random, multiple emails, press down the Ctrl key as you select emails.</li></ul>
</li><li>Choose the Delete icon on the OWA Toolbar, or right-click and select Delete.</li><li>OWA asks you to confirm your action. Choose <b>OK</b> to continue, or <b>Cancel</b> to quit the Permanent Delete operation. When <b>OK</b> is selected, there is no chance for email recovery (unless a server backup was performed before the email was permanently deleted).<br /><em>Result:</em> The Delete confirmation window displays.</li></ol>
<h2><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Permanently Delete Email from Options &gt; Deleted Items</span></h2>
<ol><li>Select <b>Options &gt; Deleted Items</b>.</li><li>On the Deleted Items page, select the email you wish to delete in the Recover Deleted Items scroll box. </li><li>Choose the <b>Permanently Delete</b> option above the scroll box.</li><li>OWA asks you to confirm your action. Choose <b>OK</b> to continue, or <b>Cancel</b> to quit the Permanent Delete operation. When <b>OK</b> is selected, there is no chance for email recovery (unless a server backup was performed before the email was permanently deleted).</li></ol>
<h2><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Automatically Empty Deleted Items on Log Off</span></h2>
<p>OWA can be configured to empty Deleted Items when the user logs off of OWA. The items will be stored in the Recover Deleted Items scroll box for 14 days.</p>
<ol><li>Select <b>Options &gt; Deleted Items</b>.</li><li>Check the <b>Empty Deleted Items folder on logoff</b> checkbox.</li><li>Click <b>Save</b> to save your changes.</li></ol>
